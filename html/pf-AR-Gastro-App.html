UI and Interaction Design for AR-Applications in the Gastronomy Sector

Although today there is a huge variety of restaurants, cafes and dining options, many people wonder where to go for lunch or dinner.

  • Often there are many restaurants in the area, but you can eat at your favorite Italian restaurant.

  • If you want to try something new, this usually ends in a long search on several recommendation portals like TripAdvisor or Yelp.

What is the goal?

An uncomplicated and simple search for dining options tailored to the individual and temporary interests of the user using augmented reality (AR). But why AR?

The mobile augmented reality is quite young but is developing rapidly. In 2008 the first AR Browser was released with Wikitude. But also AR applications like Pokemon Go, Ikea Furniture and Giphy were first used by a broad mass of users on their mobile devices.

In our prototype, we go one step further and imagine the user utilizes an AR-glasses such as one introduced by Google. This allows the user to test augmented reality on a new level and also explore the environment in the gastro area.

Target Group

The short interviews revealed the following needs of the target group:

  • Nice atmosphere, good quality food, filters by price

  • The distance of the restaurant, reviews, authentic pictures of the food in the menu.

  • Filter the Kitchen, Individual Discounts, Minimalist Design.

Therefore, our target group primerly constists of users who...

  • like to go out to eat and try out new restaurants

  • People traveling or in a foreign city / environment.

  • Age: approx. 16 - 65

High-Fidelity Prototyp

Part of the contextual scenario of our personas. The red area shows what is covered in the high-fidelity prototype. Here it will focus on the user tasks "find a restaurant" and the "way to the restaurant".

USER STORY: Find a restaurant for lunch

  • Filter the kitchen

  • filter the distance

  • filter prices

  • display opening times

  • current visitor strength

  • current actions

  • optional menu cards

The user is then guided to the chosen restaurant, where the total distance is displayed by arrows leading the direction to the user's destination.

Design

A clear typo with simple graphics should be used. Information should be limited to the essentials. The less and simpler, the better. Hence there will be also less active interaction and more automated ads, that are mainly informative. Highlighting should emphasize more important information.

Good and bad design example. The second picture shows an overloaded view with irritating information.

Final Mock-Up

The design was kept clear and simple. The user can navigate through a rotating menu bar in the lower field of view. The selection is made by tapping or swiping with the hand.
