Eatly App Conception

As part of the module E-Business at Beuth Hochschule Berlin, a very own project idea was researched, analyzed, designed and partly created.

Eatly is an app that proposes recipes individually. The app should help to eat healthier in everyday life. As the name suggests Eatly, the app is composed of the components of "healthy food" and "time" or rather time management to promote a healthier lifestyle.

Target Group

  • Primarily active and/or working people who value balanced nutrition and would like to save time and effort while shopping.

  • Especially people who follow special diet plans or diets (vegan/vegetarian, protein-rich, raw, food combining, etc.)

  • People who most likely already have experience with online shopping.

  • Age: approx. 19 - 55

Minimum Viable Product

Essential core functionalities are the following features:

  • Recipe suggestions that are individually adapted to the daily / weekly schedule.

  • App proposes recipes with regard to a personal nutrition plan or certain nutritional principles and time schedule of the user.

  • A (weekly) shopping list is created directly from the selected recipes.

  • Delivery- Order directly via the app

Product Market

There are many recipes and nutritional apps on the market, but very few that allow daily/weekly planning.

Apps like Yummly, Kitchen Stories, KptnCook are best for finding recipes. However, it is usually only possible to save the recipes in your own cookbooks/recipes collections and not in a proper daily/weekly schedule.

Although lesser-known apps like Meal Maker, EasyMenu or Mealime already allow planning of the daily or weekly menu, they are very expensive to use and only limited in terms of nutrition, diets etc adjustable.

Furthermore, there are applications such as Low Carb Menu Planner, Keto diet and Vegan Recipes, which are designed for the implementation of a specific diet plan and thus have a very specialized but also a limited range of recipes.
